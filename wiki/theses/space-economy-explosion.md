## Thesis Statement

Cost-per-kilogram to orbit has dropped roughly an order of magnitude in fifteen years and will drop another order of magnitude with Starship. When transportation cost collapses, asset accumulation explodes — this is the historical pattern with shipping, rail, and bandwidth, and there is no reason to believe space is different. SpaceX is the obvious winner and probably the largest. The investable surface includes constellation operators, in-space services, downstream data products, and second-order infrastructure (orbital data centers, in-space manufacturing, lunar logistics) that emerges when space becomes routine. Most value is being created in private companies; the rerate to public when these IPO will be one of the defining trades of the decade.

## Why Now / Structural Drivers

1. **Starship architecture transitions launch from artisanal to industrial.** Full reusability changes the cost curve by another 10x. Even if Starship slips, Falcon 9 + Falcon Heavy already collapse the cost basis below historical comparables.
2. **Starlink demonstrates constellation economics work.** ARPU at scale is the question; the technical/cost barriers are answered.
3. **Defense reorientation toward space.** Space Force, allied procurement, intelligence customers. Defense primes are diluted; pure-plays capture better margins.
4. **Second-order applications are pre-IPO.** Vast (space stations), Varda (in-space pharma), Stoke (rapid-reusability launch), Anduril (defense), all building toward IPO windows in this decade.

## Sub-Theses

### 4.1 — SpaceX is the Largest Private Asset of the Decade

Probably the largest. The rerate when (if) SpaceX IPOs is a defining event for retail investors. Access today is Forge/EquityZen/Hiive at secondary discounts. Exposure here is a portfolio decision, not a stock pick.

### 4.2 — Public Proxies Are Weak But Tradeable

RKLB is the cleanest pure-play public launcher with credible execution. PL/MAXR/ASTS are partial expressions of constellation and observation. Defense primes (LMT, NOC, RTX) are diluted but pick up DoD space spend. Position sizing should reflect that no public name is a perfect proxy.

### 4.3 — In-Space Manufacturing and Orbital Compute Are the Next Frontier

Once launch is cheap and routine, the economics of doing things in orbit change qualitatively. Pharmaceuticals (Varda), data centers (Starcloud and successors), lunar resource extraction. This is 7-15 year, not 3-5 year.

### 4.4 — IPO Wave is the Defining Trade Window

Watch for: SpaceX IPO (timing speculative; could be never), Anduril public listing, Stoke first revenue milestone, Varda commercial product launch. The rerate window is when these enter public markets.

## How This Could Be Wrong

Starship could continue to slip materially. Constellation economics may turn out worse than current Starlink-extrapolation suggests. Regulatory pushback on orbital congestion may slow asset deployment. The thesis horizon may extend beyond ten years, in which case position sizing needs to reflect duration risk. The biggest practical risk: most of the value is private, and retail access via secondary markets carries discounts/fees that reduce realized returns even if the thesis is right.
