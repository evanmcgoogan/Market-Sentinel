## Thesis Statement

Memory is being repriced from cyclical to exponential, and almost no one in public markets has internalized what that means. The driver is not consumer electronics — it is long-context agents and embodied robotics requiring persistent and experiential memory orders of magnitude beyond chatbot sessions. The same logic propagates up the entire stack: compute demand is bottlenecked at fabrication (TSMC), fabrication at lithography (ASML), lithography at materials (China-dominated). Every link can throttle the others, and Jevons paradox applies — cheaper intelligence increases intelligence demand geometrically.

## Why Now / Structural Drivers

1. **Memory demand profile changed in 2024-2025.** Agents that run for weeks, not minutes, need persistent memory. Humanoid robots need experiential memory they can recall and share. Memory required per unit of intelligence is growing faster than the intelligence itself.
2. **The bottleneck stack is rigid.** ASML → TSM → SK Hynix/Micron → equipment layer (AMAT/KLAC/LRCX/TER) → materials (China). Each link is replaceable only over 5-10 year horizons.
3. **Aggregate spend has tripled in three years and remains early.** AI capex by hyperscalers crossed $300B annual run-rate. Frontier labs continue raising at terms that imply more, not less.
4. **Jevons is observable.** Inference per dollar has dropped 100x in three years. Aggregate inference spend has gone up, not down.

## Sub-Theses

### 1.1 — Memory Rerate from Cyclical to Secular

Memory names (Hynix, Micron) deserve durable growth multiples. The market is mid-rerate. Watch for HBM ASP trajectory, capex guidance from Hynix/Micron/Samsung, and hyperscaler memory procurement disclosures.

**Specific positions:** MU (US-listed proxy), SK Hynix (000660.KS, more leveraged but harder access), Samsung (005930.KS, conglomerate dilution).

### 1.2 — Equipment Layer is Structurally Underowned

AMAT, KLAC, LRCX, TER trade at discounts to TSM and ASML despite being part of the same bottleneck stack. Retail underowns. Sentiment treats them as cyclical.

### 1.3 — Materials Layer is the Most Overlooked Piece

Refined gallium, germanium, neon, photoresists, high-purity silicon. China dominates most, Russia/Ukraine some. The "picks and shovels of the picks and shovels." Investment expression overlaps with Multipolarity thesis (see convergence: [[Sovereignty over the Material Layer]]).

### 1.4 — Inference > Training Going Forward

Training compute is bounded by frontier lab count and CAPEX. Inference compute is unbounded — every agent, every robot, every drug-discovery iteration consumes inference. Inference-optimized silicon (custom ASICs, AMD MI series, eventually Cerebras/Groq if public) is the durable demand layer.

## How This Could Be Wrong

A discontinuous architectural advance (something post-Transformer, an order of magnitude more efficient per parameter) could collapse demand projections faster than supply can adjust. Conservative answer: Jevons still applies to inference at scale. Aggressive answer: the world model must track architectural research closely enough to catch this if it happens. The arXiv ingestion pipeline (cs.AI, cs.LG, cs.NE categories + Karpathy/Sutskever/LeCun watchlist) is the early-warning system for this falsifier.
