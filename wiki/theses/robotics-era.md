## Thesis Statement

Robotics will be the largest industry in human history. The reasoning is mechanical: robotics is the conversion of intelligence into physical work. Intelligence is now cheap and getting cheaper. Physical work, in aggregate, is the largest cost category that exists across every economy on Earth. Two structural moves follow: (1) manufacturing scale beats intellectual property, and (2) humanoid form factor is a transition layer, not the endgame — purpose-built robotics dominate once the world is redesigned for agents.

## Why Now / Structural Drivers

1. **Foundation models now generalize across embodiments.** What used to require years of per-task training now transfers within months. The marginal cost of a new robot task is collapsing.
2. **Tesla, Figure, Unitree, and Chinese manufacturers are converging on production economics.** First commercial units shipping at $30-50K BOM. Path to <$20K within thesis horizon is credible.
3. **Demographics force the adoption curve.** Aging populations across developed and East Asian economies create non-discretionary demand for physical labor automation.
4. **The Dark Factory endgame is visible.** Tesla Gigafactories are the first instance — power, compute, production co-located. Convergence with [[AI Infrastructure Supercycle]] and [[Energy Abundance Supercycle]].

## Sub-Theses

### 2.1 — Manufacturing Scale Beats Intellectual Property

This is contrarian in venture capital but obvious in industrial history. Automobile winners weren't patent holders; they were the companies that could build at scale. Smartphone winners weren't original designers; they were manufacturers and ecosystem operators. The robotics era rewards the same pattern. Tesla is one obvious candidate. They will not be the only one, and may not be the largest. Watch Chinese industrial base carefully.

### 2.2 — Humanoid is a Transition Layer

Humanoid robots dominate the first wave because (a) the world is designed for human bodies, so humanoids slot in, and (b) humans can demonstrate tasks, accelerating training data accumulation. Neither reason is permanent. Once the world is redesigned for agents (as it was redesigned for cars in the 20th century), purpose-built robotics dominate.

### 2.3 — The Endgame is Dark Factories

Power plant + data center + production line + ASI orchestration, co-located, energy-symbiotic. Each component exists today. None co-located at scale. The first generation appears within the thesis horizon. See convergence: [[Dark Factory Triad]].

### 2.4 — Industrial Automation Incumbents Capture Material Share

Fanuc, ABB, Rockwell, Siemens may capture more value than the robot-native companies expect. They have customer relationships, integration expertise, and global service networks that startups cannot replicate quickly. This is a hedge against the disruptive narrative.

## How This Could Be Wrong

Training data for embodied intelligence may scale worse than current researchers expect. Humanoid form factor may turn out commercially unworkable at consumer price points. Industrial automation incumbents may capture more of the value than disruptive startups. None of these invalidate the thesis; they shift the winners. The bigger risk is timing — the thesis horizon is 5-15 years, and capital allocation discipline matters: don't pay 2040 multiples in 2026.
