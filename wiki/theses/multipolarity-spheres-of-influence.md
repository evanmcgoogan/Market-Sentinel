## Thesis Statement

We are transitioning from a unipolar to a multipolar world organized around four functional spheres of influence: the United States, China, Russia, and Israel. Europe sits in the US sphere and is structurally declining despite a defense and energy reawakening. Each sphere has asymmetric strengths and weaknesses, and capital flows accordingly. Taiwan reunification is inevitable; timing is the uncertainty. USD reserve currency erosion is real but slow. Onshoring and friend-shoring is a structural decade-long CAPEX cycle. The intellectual genealogy is Ray Dalio's *Changing World Order* + historical empire transitions, with Marko Papic's work on geopolitical alpha as the trading layer.

## Why Now / Structural Drivers

1. **Observable, not forecast.** Central bank gold purchases (record levels 2022-2025). BRICS settlement experiments. Chinese export controls on rare earths. US LNG dominance. Russian crude flowing eastward at discount. Steady chipping of petrodollar arrangement.
2. **CHIPS Act, IRA, EU industrial policy.** Multi-trillion dollar capital flows toward capacity within friendly geographies. This is the friend-shoring CAPEX cycle in flight.
3. **Defense spending across NATO is rising, not falling.** First time in decades. Germany alone added €100B explicit. US baseline up. Pacific allies (Japan, AU, KR) following.
4. **Crypto/BTC as digital reserve asset thesis becoming institutional.** Spot ETFs in US, allocations from sovereigns (Norway, MicroStrategy as proxy for corporate). USD-replacement narrative measurable.

## The Four Spheres

### United States
- **Strengths:** Ingenuity, culture/entertainment exports, military, leading-edge tech and services, capital markets, energy independence.
- **Weaknesses:** Political polarization, debt trajectory, declining manufacturing base (correcting through reshoring), demographics.
- **Investment expression:** Reshoring CAPEX names (steel, semis equipment, transformer), defense primes, AI infrastructure, energy independence.

### China
- **Strengths:** Mining and materials refining, manufacturing dominance, scale, infrastructure execution, increasingly competitive tech (DeepSeek and onward).
- **Weaknesses:** Demographic collapse, real estate overhang, capital outflows, IP dependence on West for highest-end fabrication.
- **Investment expression:** Avoid most direct China equity. Indirect exposure via US-listed beneficiaries of cheap China inputs.

### Russia
- **Strengths:** Energy production and reserves, weapons (proven in Ukraine attrition), grain, traditional values anchor.
- **Weaknesses:** Brain drain, demographics, sanctions overhang, deep dependence on China as economic counterparty.
- **Investment expression:** Mostly not investable directly. Energy implications captured via [[Energy Abundance Supercycle]].

### Israel
- **Strengths:** Military technology, cyber capability, US-Israel collaboration, defense exports, Unit 8200 alumni networks, biotech.
- **Weaknesses:** Regional isolation, demographic divisions, dependence on US alliance.
- **Investment expression:** Israeli cyber and defense names (CYBR, CHKP), select biotech.

## Sub-Theses

### 6.1 — Taiwan Reunification is Inevitable on Uncertain Timing

The direction is not the uncertainty. The timing is. Position sizing on TSM, ASML, and the equipment layer should be calibrated to a non-trivial probability of disruption within thesis horizon. This is not a "sell TSM" call — it's a "size TSM to survive a Taiwan event without portfolio ruin" call.

### 6.2 — Russian Consolidation Toward Former-USSR Sphere Continues

Ukraine settles with concessions. Russian economic and military influence in Caucasus, Central Asia, and arguably Levant expands. Investment expression: oil/gas global rebalancing, defense baseline elevated.

### 6.3 — USD Reserve Erosion is Real but Slow

Historical reserve transitions take generations. The investment expression (gold, hard assets, EM ex-China, decentralized monetary systems, Bitcoin as digital reserve asset) compounds over decades. Size accordingly — this is a structural hedge, not a tactical trade.

### 6.4 — Onshoring/Friend-Shoring is a Structural Decade-Long CAPEX Cycle

CHIPS Act, IRA, Mexican FDI inflows, EU industrial policy. Capital flows to capacity within friendly geographies. Direct beneficiaries: domestic semis equipment, friend-shored materials processing, US/CA/AU/MX manufacturing buildout.

## How This Could Be Wrong

Multipolar transitions can resolve back to bipolar (US vs China dominant, Russia and Israel as regional powers) rather than fully four-sphere. Reserve currency erosion can take longer than the thesis horizon. Investable expressions of multipolarity (defense, hard assets, energy sovereignty) can be priced ahead of fundamentals. The biggest practical risk: this thesis is slow-moving, and reading the news daily can create false urgency that leads to overtrading.
